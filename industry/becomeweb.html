Become a Web Developer

A lucrative field with a lot to know.

Instructions:

Watch this video and then discuss with you classmates how many of the terms you know and then do bit of research for items you did not know about and provide links to articles and/or video(s) you find.


My response:

While I greatly appreciated the thoroughness of this presentation, it really made me realize just how much I still had to learn. After about the first ten minutes my brain sort of got lost in the information overload, and it took me a couple days to research and get through the rest of the video. I’ll share some highlights of my research, focusing on the things I would like to learn in the immediate future (thank golly for Lynda.com).

  • jQuery: My interest was immediately piqued with the mention of “design”, so of course I had to check this out. Despite making it sound like there wasn’t much potential for growth, I still decided to look into it, and I found this interesting site. Sure, a lot of things you can do with plain JavaScript, but look how easy jQuery makes it!

  • RESTful Web Services: Never heard of the term before but it’s a very basic concept, and I found a helpful web resource to explain it better.

  • Bootstrap: I’ll admit that I really need to brush up on CSS. To me, CSS has always been a bit harder to understand than HTML. Luckily there are these neat things called frameworks to help you along. There’s also a Lynda.com tutorial about Bootstrap that I’m going to check out.

  • React.js: Being completely new to JavaScript, I didn’t really even know what a framework was. This article helped me gain a better understanding, and from there this site was a good little introduction to React.
