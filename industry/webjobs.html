My response:

Some of the keywords I used in my search were:

  • Fresno, CA
  • web developer
  • front-end
  • back-end
  • full-stack
  • web programmer
  • web application developer
  • nonprofit (just tagged on at the end of any of the above terms)

    • I used Google, Indeed.com and Craigslist for my search. I also changed my location search area to San Francisco just out of curiosity, and while there may be a good amount of web development jobs in Fresno, the amount of openings in the San Francisco Bay area entirely dwarfs Fresno by comparison. I was not surprised in the least, considering San Francisco is next door to Silicon Valley and is consistently rated as one of the top cities for web development jobs. There weren't many postings on Craigslist in general, but here is a comparison of my results. Taking all this into consideration, I would still rather work in Fresno as the cost of living is much lower here!


    Plugging the term "nonprofit" into my search yielded virtually no jobs in the Fresno area, but there are a few jobs out there. I stumbled upon an interesting article about how web developers could potentially hurt the nonprofit they do work for. For example, making changes extremely difficult or impossible for the organization's staff to do themselves is just sort of... mean. Nonprofits usually don't have the budget to hire on someone to handle all their web content needs, so giving them the autonomy to do it themselves is essential. For this, the author of the article suggests implementing a CMS like WordPress. Several years ago I made a website for my friend who was an amateur voice actor, and I went with a CMS for ease of use and cost.
