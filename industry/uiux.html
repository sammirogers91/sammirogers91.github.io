My response:

As a digital artist I already have a pretty solid grasp of what a User Interface is. User Experience, on the other hand, is a relatively new term that I've discovered only in recent years. I initially thought a UX designer was just another term for a front-end developer. In some regards this can technically be true, depending on the size of the organization (think of a large company like a clothing retailer versus a one-man-team freelancer) and the delegation of duties, but a UX designer in their "purest" form is someone who works with the people aspect of a website. A UI designer is focused on making the website look pretty using principles of visual design and a trained eye, whereas a UX designer is the person doing analytics and trying to get more visitors to not only visit the website, but follow through with a purchase (or whatever the end goal of the business is). A User Experience designer aims to make the User Experience enjoyable and pain-free for the end user.


I found this video that I think does a good job thoroughly looking at the career of an actual UX designer for the company True&Co. In the video, the designer mentions many of the things related to this week's SEO lecture, like how she uses Google Analytics and does A/B testing on the job. I feel that UX designers actually take on a bit of a management and marketing role in their duties, compared to UI designers who largely are more concerned with the artistic side of things.
