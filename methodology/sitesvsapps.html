The key difference lies in functionality.

My response:


Site vs. App

I will admit that I call just about everything with a URL a website. YouTube? Website. Facebook? Website. This bad habit has been fed by ignoring technicalities for years upon years and living in blissful ignorance. I do, however, recognize the differences between a website and a web app. A website is static, simple, and unchanging, with the end goal being to deliver information about a product or service quickly and easily. A front-end web developer would have no problem creating a website.


A web application, on the other hand, is dynamic and changes rapidly, and has the capabilities to handle advanced user interaction in order to deliver a customized experience for each user. In addition to using front-end technologies, web apps are built and maintained with programming languages, databases, and frameworks. A full stack developer would be the person I would hire to build a web application for me.



"Whatever good things we build end up building us."

I may have a need for a portfolio website sometime in the near future. Ideally I would like to have my own domain name, because for years I have been using a site (or web app??) called DeviantArt. Frankly, DeviantArt has become a cesspool of crud over the years, with no real way to filter past the thousands of submissions of edgy anime characters drawn on binder paper by 12 year-olds, to get to the professional artists who... well, do art for a living. I know this sounds snobby, and I once was a kid drawing anime characters on binder paper, but if I show a potential client a link to my DeviantArt portolio, there are a lot of distractions on the page that can be avoided by having a sleek, clean, professional website.


Other than that, I don't really have need or know anyone in need of a web app. I decided to look up a few "beginner" projects that I'm keen to check out.
