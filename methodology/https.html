HTTPS

The importance of having secure web content.

Instructions:

Hello Everyone!


This is a great talk about HTTPS @ Google I/O this year, on HTTPS! I think the entire talk is worth your time, but watch at least half of the talk. Then reply with your thoughts on the information covered and/or research any of the concepts that are new to you.


My response:


I wasn't planning on watching the entire thing, but I ended up finishing it in two sittings. This is really important stuff to know, and while a lot of the topics covered were things I was familiar with, I decided to do my own research on a few things that caught my interest.


For the fun of it, I checked out SSLLabs.com and ran a few sites through their quality check. Most sites, as expected, turned out an A rating, however WebAdvisor ended up with a big, fat F. I decided to check it out using the developer tools in Chrome, and it came up with a much more positive assessment with the exception of using an obsolete cipher (which was also noted in SSLLabs' assessment. Upon further inspection, and I may not be interpreting the information correctly because I'm still a noob, I learned that SSLLabs assigned the site an F because of alleged susceptibility to something known as the DROWN vulnerability.


Back to the video, I got sort of lost in the information (I guess I may have zoned out a few times) and so at the end I looked up how to secure your site using HTTPS and found Google's nice little instructional piece. that sort of summarizes a lot of what was explained in the presentation. This is something every developer should know how to do and know why it's so important.
