My response:


I had a lot of fun doing this assignment.


Before and after.


I started out with a pretty massive stock image that I grabbed from good ol' DeviantArt.com (Links to an external site.)Links to an external site. to be sure there were no licensing issues as long as I followed the photographer's terms (which I did). In summary, the original image was a whopping 11.6 MB file at 5120x3427 dimensions and a DPI of 300. So, your typical high-quality stock photo. After butchering it in Photoshop CC 2017, I ended up with a 50.4 KB image at 768x271 and a DPI of 72.


So! The first thing I did after loading it into my editing program was change the DPI from 300 to 72. This also shrunk the image's dimensions, which was fine. I then cropped out the (roughly) top and bottom thirds, leaving a portion to edit. Normally I would do all my editing at a DPI but I didn't think to edit it until after I resized it, so I just went with what I had.


First, to get rid of some of the graininess, I ran the blur tool over the larger blobby bits of the clouds. After, I made a copy of the layer on top of the main layer and set the opacity to 40% and the layer blending mode to overlay, giving the image some more depth. To further reduce the noise I ran a Gaussian blur filter over the overlay layer, and on that same layer I played with the color balance to give the highlights and midtones some warmth into what I thought was a pretty boring, monochromatic color scheme.


Lastly I resized the image down to 768x271, saved it as a jpeg and compressed it a little to cut down on file size. It sounds like a lot of work, but the whole process took about 5 minutes.
