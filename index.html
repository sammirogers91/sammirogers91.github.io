The Path Forward

I think a little bit of backstory is needed before I tell you which direction I’m going from here.


Three months ago, I hit rock bottom or somewhere close. I had failed so many classes (after being a straight-A student) that I dropped out of school. I failed an internship. I was burnt-out, jobless, ashamed that I had failed to live up to my own expectations, and at my lowest I didn’t want to live anymore.


It took me an entire year and a half to really turn my focus inward and reflect. It was then I discovered (or really, rediscovered) some things about myself that I had neglected. Namely, I realized I had been starving my inner creativity. In my pursuit of a degree that I had no passion in, in pursuit of a life that didn’t match up to my personality, values and inner rhythm, I had lost myself.


I was so scared of following my passions, I was so focused on this image of a “rich, successful accountant” that I had thrown away my happiness to achieve what? This false ideal of security. And it took away almost everything I had worked so hard for.


I decided to get back into art and build upon my creative talent. I’m not sure what the catalyst was other than I decided I was tired of being unhappy. As I began to improve my drawing and my writing, and as I began to learn new skills like 3D animation, I started feeling good about myself again. I thought: why stop there?


Then I enrolled in Fresno City College, with the intent of taking some graphic design classes. On a whim, as I was selecting my courses I happened to check out the web development courses. On a whim, I enrolled in server-side scripting. And, on a whim, I enrolled in this class.


Now it’s my major and I can SEE myself doing this as a career.


I can’t really put into words how much my outlook on life has changed. I’m not the best. I probably won’t be the best at web development. But I’m going to try damn hard to learn, because I’m passionate about it.


It’s never too late to begin a new path in life, and I’m so very grateful for all of the supportive people in my life who believed in me before I even believed in myself.
