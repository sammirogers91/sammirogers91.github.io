Web Hosting

There are many different options for hosting your website, depending on your needs.

Instructions:

There are many options for hosting website these days! Do some research and find a free and a paid option and post link for your fellow classmates to review. Write 150 about what you found in your research and/or experience and do two substantive replies. Here is a basic video about web hosting.


My response:


There is a surprising number of free web hosting plans out there. Sure, most of the websites look a little dated and have questionable English and offer very little support for a wide variety of scripting languages and databases, but it's free! For a very small business that doesn't engage in e-commerce, free hosting might be the way to go.


Let's start with WebFreeHosting, my pick (not my favorite by any means) for this discussion. For their free plans, they offer a gig of disk space, allow 5 gigs of traffic per month, and even give you a free e-mail. Granted, I can't find many reviews of them on the web except for this totally not sponsored blog review, and I can't find anything substantial to backup their claims that their servers run 100% off of wind power, but that's pretty neat if it's true.


On the paid end of the spectrum, I decided to talk about good ol' GoDaddy. Back when I was a web neophyte, I used GoDaddy for a number of sites and domain name registration. Looking back, a simple Google search for a better host probably would have saved me from enduring the massive amounts of pain and tears caused by being an unfortunate customer to GoDaddy's god awful customer service and hosting methodology. But I was young, dumb, and my grandfather's love of NASCAR meant I was exposed to constant GoDaddy advertisement.


Speaking of ads, there are ads everywhere on GoDaddy (at least there was back in 2009). Their back-end user area was atrocious and I wasted a considerable amount of time trying to find what should have been the most basic functions. And it was covered in ads. Pretty soon, my mailbox (yes, my physical mailbox outside my home) became stuffed full of ads as well, and to this day my grandparents still receive advertisements relating to a domain name I haven't owned for 8 years.
